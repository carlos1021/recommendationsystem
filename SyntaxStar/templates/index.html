What is AP Physics 1 and why should we care?

AP Physics 1, also known as Advanced Placement Physics 1, is a high school-level physics course designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of fundamental concepts in physics. It is typically taken by high school students who are looking for a more challenging and advanced physics curriculum. The course covers a wide range of topics in mechanics, including:

Kinematics
Dynamics
Circular Motion and Gravitation
Energy
Momentum
Simple Harmonic Motion
Torque and Rotational Motion
Electric Charges and Electric Force

Units

Unit 1: Kinematics

The study of motion, including concepts like displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Read More
Unit 2: Dynamics

The study of the forces and interactions that cause objects to move and how those forces affect an object's motion. It includes principles such as Newton's Laws of Motion.

Read More
Unit 3: Circular Motion and Gravitation

The study of objects moving in circular paths and the gravitational force that governs the motion of objects in the universe.

Read More
Unit 4: Energy

The study of the capacity of objects to do work or transfer heat, encompassing concepts like kinetic energy, potential energy, and the conservation of energy.

Read More
Unit 5: Momentum

The quantity of motion possessed by an object, defined as the product of its mass and velocity, and a key concept in understanding how objects interact and move in collisions and interactions.

Read More
Unit 6: Simple Harmonic Motion

The repetitive back-and-forth motion exhibited by objects when a restoring force proportional to their displacement from a central point acts on them, often observed in systems like pendulums and mass-spring systems.
